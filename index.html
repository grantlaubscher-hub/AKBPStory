Chapter 01 / 6:04 AM

The door opened onto a different world

The house still held the small, ordinary evidence of yesterday: a mug beside the sink, a pair of sandals by the back door, Bo Pig’s lead hanging from its hook. Inside, everything was quiet enough to seem staged.

Outside, Banksia Beach had lost its colour. The palms stood black against a bruised sky. Fine ash drifted across the driveway and settled on the roof of Alex’s car, soft as flour, grey as bone. Far off, somewhere toward the Passage, something metal knocked slowly in the wind.

Bo Pig stepped out first. His merle coat looked almost blue in the dead morning light. He lowered his nose to the ash, breathed in the changed world, and gave one uncertain wag of his tail.

Chapter 02 / 3:17 AM

The war had passed without a face

No one had seen an army. No one had heard engines. The war came as a pressure behind the eyes, as a low pulse beneath the floorboards, as a pale flash that turned the clouds white from the inside.

At 3:17, every clock stopped. Every screen went black. The windows shivered in their frames, then bowed inward as if the whole night had taken a breath against the glass.

By dawn, the neighbourhood was intact but emptied of explanation. The houses remained. The letterboxes remained. The streets remained. What was missing was the sound of people believing tomorrow would arrive as expected.

The world had ended without explaining itself. Bo Pig, who had never trusted explanations, simply kept walking.

Chapter 03 / The first kilometre

Past the canals and burnt banksias

Alex followed the white flash of Bo Pig’s tail through streets that were almost familiar. A sprinkler ticked over a dead lawn. A bicycle lay on its side in the gutter. A curtain moved in an upstairs window, though there was no breeze strong enough to move it.

The banksias along the verge had split open in the heat, their seed pods cracked like little black mouths. Bo Pig sniffed each one with grave professional interest, then looked back at Alex, impatient with grief, ready for the next clue.

Alex counted her steps because counting was easier than calling out. She counted the driveways, the abandoned cars, the silent houses. She counted Bo Pig’s paw prints where they appeared in the ash and disappeared again on the bitumen.

Chapter 04 / The far shore

The water was still there

At the old jetty, the world became wider. The Pumicestone Passage moved under the ruined sky with the same patient rhythm it had carried yesterday, and the day before, and every day Alex had mistaken for permanent.

She sat on the boards and finally let herself shake. Bo Pig pressed his warm body against her leg. There was ash on his whiskers and salt in the fur under his chin. He watched the horizon as if he had been expecting it to speak.

Then, across the water, something flashed once. Not lightning. Not fire. A deliberate point of light from the far shore. Alex stood. Bo Pig was already standing, ears forward, body still, every instinct aimed at the impossible fact of someone else alive.

So they walked toward the light.

Alex with ash on her cheeks. Bo Pig with salt on his whiskers. Behind them, the quiet house waited in Banksia Beach. Ahead, the broken world held its breath, and somewhere across the Passage, someone was still alive enough to shine.
